🥒 Crisp Hydroponic Cucumbers

A fast-growing vine crop. Requires trellising support and yields refreshing cucumbers in under 2 months.

📈 Overview & Growing Requirements

Optimal pH Range 5.5 - 6.0
EC Range (Nutrient level) 1.6 - 2.5 mS/cm
Growth Cycle Time 50 - 70 Days
Preferred System DWC or Drip Systems
Water Temperature 20°C - 23°C
Daily Light Requirement 14 - 16 Hours

📋 Step-by-Step Cultivation Guide

1

Seed Incubation (Days 1–5)

Cucumbers germinate quickly. Seed them directly in rockwool. Keep warm (25°C) and moist. Sprout roots will seek water within days.

2

Transfer to Growth Bed (Days 6–10)

Once true leaves appear, transplant the seedling plug into your deep water culture net cup or setup near a drip line. Allow roots to dangle down.

3

Vine Training & Pruning (Days 10–40)

Provide a trellis. As the main vine grows, tie it loosely. Remove early suckers and male flowers (if not self-pollinating variety) to strengthen roots.

4

Flowering & Harvesting (Days 40–70)

Most commercial varieties are parthenocarpic (don't need pollination). Pick cucumbers when they reach 6–8 inches for best crunch and taste.
