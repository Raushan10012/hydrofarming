📚 Hydroponics Learning Center

Learn everything about hydroponic farming from beginner to advanced level.

What is Hydroponics?

Hydroponics is a method of growing plants without soil, instead using a continuous flow or pool of nutrient-rich water directly to plant roots.

NFT System

NFT (Nutrient Film Technique) continuously flows a thin stream of nutrient-rich water through channels, feeding roots directly and recirculating water back to a reservoir.

DWC System

Deep Water Culture (DWC) keeps plant roots completely submerged in an oxygenated nutrient water bath, kept aerated via air pumps and bubblers.

Aeroponics

In aeroponic setups, plant roots are suspended in air and misted automatically at regular intervals with specialized nutrient solutions.

Nutrient Management

Hydroponic plants require specific concentrations of Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), Potassium (K), and trace minerals dissolved in water.

pH Management

Most hydroponic crops absorb nutrients best in slightly acidic ranges between pH 5.5 and 6.5. Adjust with pH Up or pH Down solutions.

Best Crops For Beginners

Lettuce, basil, spinach, mint, and kale are fast growing, forgiving, and excellent crops for beginners starting their first system.

Common Problems

Watch out for algae growth (caused by light entering reservoirs), root rot (due to poor aeration/water temperature), and nutrient deficiencies.

Pest Control

Maintain proper airflow and screen entries. Treat pests with eco-friendly neem oil sprays, insecticidal soaps, or introduce beneficial predator insects.

Setup Cost

A small home DWC or Kratky system can be assembled for under ₹1500, whereas commercial multi-tier NFT systems can cost ₹5000 and upwards.
